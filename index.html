Why a grammar

The first volume ordered the vocabulary of making by domain and by thread. This one cannot be ordered that way, because its terms are not siblings — they are derivations. A diffusion model is unintelligible without the latent space it runs in; an agent is a language model with tools and goals; every critique term points back at some training decision made three layers down.

So the ordering principle here is dependency. Seven layers, each only allowed to use what the layers beneath it have already defined — the way a grammar builds sentences from clauses, clauses from words, words from letters. Click any term and the page traces its ancestry to the ground.

One rule kept the list durable: name the concept, not the product. No model names, no version numbers, no tools of the season. Every headword is a research concept with a citation trail — the layer beneath the churn.
